1) Core Concept: Useful Facts and Formulae

Must Know

Useful Facts and Formulae is a key pillar of Surface Tension in NEET Physics. Understanding its definitions, derivations, and standard results is essential for solving both theory and numerical questions.

  • Charged soap bubble: size increases due to outward electrostatic force
  • Water proofing agents: increase angle of contact
  • Surface tension values: Mercury 0.465 N/m, Water 0.075 N/m, Glycerine 0.063 N/m, Soap solution 0.030 N/m
Example (NEET-style)Formula: T = P₀(c³ - a³ - b³)/[4(a² + b² - c²)], surface tension from bubble coalescence

2) NEET Traps to Avoid

Exam Alert

NEET setters target conceptual gaps in Useful Facts and Formulae. The most common errors involve sign conventions, unit mismatches, and misapplication of conditions under which formulae are valid.

  • Two bubbles of radii r₁ and r₂ touching: pressure difference ΔP = 4T[1/r₁ - 1/r₂]

Useful Facts and Formulae in US vs Indian Curriculum

Properties of matter (elasticity, surface tension, fluid mechanics) receive minimal coverage in AP Physics 1. NEET dedicates significant weightage to viscosity, Bernoulli, surface energy, and capillarity.

  • NEET expects derivation-level mastery; AP Physics often treats Useful Facts and Formulae as applied only.
  • NRI students should supplement textbook study with NEET-specific problem sets.
  • Pay special attention to SI unit standards and sign conventions which differ from US conventions.
