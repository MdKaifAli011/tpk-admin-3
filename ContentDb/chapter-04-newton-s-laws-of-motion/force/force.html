Force – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Force is an external push or pull that changes or tends to change the state of rest or motion, direction of motion, or shape/size of a body. Dimension: [MLT⁻²]. SI unit: Newton (N); CGS: dyne; 1 N = 10⁵ dyne. Four fundamental forces in nature: Gravitational, Weak Nuclear, Electromagnetic, Strong Nuclear (order of increasing strength). Common mechanics forces include Weight (W = mg), Normal Reaction, Tension, and Spring Force (F = −Kx). NEET heavily tests force types, units, and basic force mechanics.

Quick Revision Cards

Concept → Trap → Example

1) Definition and Effects of Force

Foundation

Force is an external push or pull that produces or tries to produce motion, stops or changes motion, changes direction of motion, or changes shape of a body.

  • Dimension: [MLT⁻²]
  • SI unit: Newton (N) = 1 kg·m/s²
  • CGS unit: Dyne = 1 g·cm/s²; 1 N = 10⁵ dyne
Example (NEET-style)A bat swinging at a ball exerts force that changes the ball's direction and speed simultaneously — two effects of force acting together.

2) Unit Systems — Force

Unit Mastery

Absolute units: Newton (SI), Dyne (CGS). Gravitational units: kilogram-force, gram-force. Conversion: 1 N = 10⁵ dyne; 1 kgf = 9.8 N.

  • 1 Newton: force giving 1 m/s² to 1 kg mass
  • 1 Dyne: force giving 1 cm/s² to 1 g mass
  • 1 kgf = 9.80 N (gravitational unit)
Example (NEET-style)A 70 kg person's weight = 70 × 9.8 = 686 N ≈ 70 kgf. These are equivalent representations.

3) Four Fundamental Forces in Nature

NEET Factual

Gravitational : Weak Nuclear : Electromagnetic : Strong Nuclear = 1 : 10²⁵ : 10³⁶ : 10³⁸. Strong Nuclear is the strongest force.

  • Gravitational: weakest — acts between all masses (via Gravitons)
  • Electromagnetic: 10³⁶ × gravity — light, electricity (via Photons)
  • Strong Nuclear: strongest — binds nucleus (via pions)
Example (NEET-style)At nuclear distances (~10⁻¹⁵ m), Strong Nuclear >> Electromagnetic >> Weak Nuclear >> Gravitational. At astronomical scales, Gravitational dominates as other forces decay faster.

4) Common Forces in Mechanics

FBD Essentials

Weight W = mg (gravitational). Normal N: perpendicular to surface. Tension T: along string, away from body. Spring F = −Kx (restoring).

  • Normal force: not always == mg; depends on acceleration and angle
  • Tension is same throughout a massless inextensible string
  • Spring constant K has units N/m; F = −Kx (Hooke's Law)
Example (NEET-style)In an elevator accelerating upward at a, apparent weight = m(g + a) > mg. The normal force from the floor exceeds the gravitational weight — distinguishing N from W is critical.

Curriculum Bridging — US vs NEET

Key differences between CBSE/NEET and US AP Physics treatment of Force.

CGS and Gravitational Force Units

US AP Physics uses SI only. NEET tests CGS units (dyne) and gravitational units (kgf, gf).

  • 1 N = 10⁵ dyne — memorise this conversion
  • 1 kgf = 9.80 N; 1 gf = 980 dyne

Four Fundamental Forces Ranking

NEET explicitly tests the relative strength ordering and associated particles. US AP typically does not.

  • Order: Gravitational (weakest) < Weak Nuclear < Electromagnetic < Strong Nuclear (strongest)
  • Relative strengths: 1 : 10²⁵ : 10³⁶ : 10³⁸

Conservative vs Non-Conservative Forces

NEET classifies forces — central, conservative, non-conservative. US AP focuses on work-energy theorem without this classification.

  • Conservative: gravitational, electric, elastic (work done = 0 in round trip)
  • Non-conservative: friction, viscous — dissipate energy

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Why is positive force repulsive and negative force attractive in the convention mentioned?
When force is written without explicit direction (as a signed scalar), a positive value means the force pushes objects apart (repulsive — like two similar charges). A negative value means the force pulls objects together (attractive — like opposite charges or gravity). This sign convention encodes direction in a compact form when vector notation is dropped.
Is tension an electromagnetic force?
Yes. Tension in a string is an electromagnetic contact force, arising from intermolecular electrostatic interactions between the atoms of the string. At the macroscopic level it appears as a mechanical force, but its microscopic origin is electromagnetic.
What is the difference between absolute and gravitational units of force?
Absolute units (Newton, dyne) are defined via F = ma and are independent of the local gravitational field. Gravitational units (kgf, gf) are defined as the weight of a standard mass — 1 kgf is the force Earth's gravity exerts on 1 kg (= 9.80 N). Gravitational units change with location; absolute units do not.
