Acceleration on Inclined Plane – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Analyses the motion of a block on a smooth inclined plane under two scenarios: (1) incline at rest — a = g sinθ, derived via Newton’s 2nd Law along the plane; (2) incline accelerating horizontally at ‘b’ — a pseudo-force (mb) must be introduced, giving a = g sinθ − b cosθ. The condition for the block to remain stationary relative to an accelerating incline (b = g tanθ) is a key NEET result.

Preparation Strategy

1

Resolve Forces Along and Perpendicular to Incline For a block on a smooth incline, gravity component along slope = mg sinθ (drives motion), perpendicular component = mg cosθ (balanced by normal reaction R). Write F = ma only along the slope.

2

Master the Pseudo-Force Case When the incline itself accelerates horizontally at ‘b’, introduce pseudo-force mb directed opposite to b in the non-inertial frame. Resolve mb too: along slope = mb cosθ (opposing mg sinθ), perpendicular = mb sinθ (adding to R).

3

Memorise the Equilibrium Condition Block at rest on accelerating incline: a = g sinθ − b cosθ = 0. Therefore b = g tanθ is the horizontal acceleration needed to keep the block stationary relative to the incline.

Quick Revision Cards

Concept → Trap → Example

1) Static Incline — Rest or Constant Velocity

Core Formula

For a block on a smooth incline at rest (θ = angle of inclination): Normal reaction R = mg cosθ. Net force along incline = mg sinθ. Acceleration a = g sinθ (down the slope).

  • R = mg cosθ
  • a = g sinθ (along slope, downward)
  • Both formulas come from resolving mg into two perpendicular components relative to the incline surface
Example (NEET-style)Block on 30° smooth incline: a = 10 × sin30° = 5 m/s²; R = 10m × cos30° = 8.66m N.

2) Accelerating Incline — Pseudo-Force

NEET High Yield

When the incline accelerates horizontally with acceleration b, introduce pseudo-force mb in the direction opposite to b. Resolving along incline: a = g sinθ − b cosθ. Normal reaction: R = mg cosθ + mb sinθ.

  • a = g sinθ − b cosθ
  • R = mg cosθ + mb sinθ
  • Pseudo-force mb cosθ reduces acceleration along slope; mb sinθ increases R
Example (NEET-style)Incline at 37° accelerates right at b = g tan37°. Block is stationary relative to incline since a = g sin37° − b cos37° = 0.

3) Equilibrium on Accelerating Incline

Quick Result

For the block to remain stationary relative to the incline (no sliding): set a = 0 in a = g sinθ − b cosθ. This gives b = g tanθ.

  • b = g tanθ keeps block at rest on incline
  • This is the horizontal acceleration the system must have
  • A classic NEET result tested in direct numerical and conceptual forms
Example (NEET-style)For a 45° incline: b = g tan45° = g = 10 m/s². The incline must accelerate at 10 m/s² to keep the block stationary on it.

US Curriculum Bridging

AP Physics C covers inclined planes but the accelerating wedge with pseudo-force analysis is typically absent.

Pseudo-Force in Non-Inertial Frames

US AP Physics generally solves the accelerating wedge from the ground frame using constraint equations. NEET prefers the pseudo-force (non-inertial frame) approach, which gives the result directly.

  • Practice applying pseudo-force mb to every body in the non-inertial frame.
  • Confirm the result by solving from the ground frame using constraint equations.

Normal Reaction on Accelerating Incline

R = mg cosθ + mb sinθ is a formula many US-trained students miss. Recognise that the incline’s acceleration adds a component perpendicular to the slope.

  • Derive R from the pseudo-force FBD.
  • Verify: when b = 0, R reduces correctly to mg cosθ.

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Why is the acceleration g sinθ and not simply g on a smooth incline?
Gravity acts straight downward. Only the component along the incline surface (mg sinθ) can produce motion along the incline because the normal reaction cancels the perpendicular component (mg cosθ). The net unbalanced force = mg sinθ, giving a = g sinθ.
Why must I use a pseudo-force when the incline accelerates?
The incline is a non-inertial reference frame when it accelerates. Newton’s laws are strictly valid only in inertial frames. Adding a pseudo-force (mb opposite to incline’s acceleration) transforms the problem back into the familiar form of Newton’s 2nd law, so the same FBD technique applies.
What does b = g tanθ physically mean?
It means the entire system (incline + block) is accelerating horizontally at exactly the right rate so that the block has no tendency to slide up or down the slope. The pseudo-force precisely cancels the gravitational component along the incline.
Does the angle θ affect whether b can keep the block stationary?
Yes. As θ increases (steeper incline), b = g tanθ increases. For a vertical wall (θ = 90°), b would need to be infinite, which is physically impossible. This tells us there is a maximum slope that can be held with any finite horizontal acceleration.
