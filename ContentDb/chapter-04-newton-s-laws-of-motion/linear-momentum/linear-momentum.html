Linear Momentum – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Linear momentum is the product of a body's mass and its velocity: p = mv. It is a vector quantity with the same direction as velocity. Measured in kg·m/s (SI) or g·cm/s (CGS). Dimension: [MLT⁻¹]. Newton's Second Law is built on the rate of change of linear momentum. NEET frequently tests momentum of bodies with same momentum but different masses, and conservation scenarios.

Quick Revision Cards

Concept → Trap → Example

1) Definition and Formula

Must Know

Linear momentum is the quantity of motion in a body — the product of mass and velocity. p = mv.

  • Vector quantity — direction same as velocity
  • SI unit: kg·m/s; CGS: g·cm/s
  • Dimension: [MLT⁻¹]
Example (NEET-style)A 2 kg ball moving at 3 m/s east has momentum p = 2 × 3 = 6 kg·m/s east.

2) Same Momentum, Different Mass

NEET Trap

If p = m₁v₁ = m₂v₂ (equal momenta), then v₁/v₂ = m₂/m₁. The lighter body has greater velocity.

  • v ∝ 1/m when momentum is constant
  • More massive body moves slower for the same momentum
  • Direct NEET question type — memorise this inverse relationship
Example (NEET-style)If a 4 kg body and a 1 kg body have the same momentum, the 1 kg body moves 4× faster.

3) Proportionality Relations

Formula Focus

For a given body: p ∝ v (at constant mass). For different bodies at same velocity: p ∝ m.

  • Double the velocity → double the momentum
  • Double the mass → double the momentum (at same speed)
  • Momentum is measured by force needed to stop body in unit time
Example (NEET-style)A car (1000 kg) at 10 m/s has the same momentum as a truck (500 kg) at 20 m/s only if p₁ = p₂ — always check the numbers.

Curriculum Bridging — US vs NEET

Key differences between CBSE/NEET and US AP Physics syllabus for linear momentum.

CGS vs SI Unit Emphasis

US AP Physics uses SI exclusively; NEET tests both CGS (g·cm/s) and SI (kg·m/s) unit conversion.

  • 1 kg·m/s = 1000 g × 100 cm/s = 10⁵ g·cm/s
  • NEET unit-comparison MCQs require fluency in both systems

Dimensional Formula

US courses rarely test dimensional formula [MLT⁻¹] directly; NEET does.

  • Force = dp/dt → [F] = [MLT⁻¹]/[T] = [MLT⁻²]
  • Know dimensions of momentum vs impulse vs force

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Is momentum a scalar or vector?
Momentum is a vector quantity with the same direction as the velocity of the body. When adding momenta of multiple bodies, you must use vector addition, not simple arithmetic.
If two bodies have the same speed but different masses, which has more momentum?
The heavier body has more momentum. Since p = mv and velocity is the same, p ∝ m. The body with greater mass has proportionally greater momentum.
How is momentum measured in terms of force?
Linear momentum is also measured as the force required to stop the body in unit time. This comes from Newton's 2nd Law: F = dp/dt → p = F × t (impulse). So 1 kg·m/s = 1 N·s.
