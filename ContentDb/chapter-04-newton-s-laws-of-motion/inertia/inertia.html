Inertia – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Inertia is the inherent property of all bodies by virtue of which they resist any change in their state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line. It is NOT a physical quantity — it has no units, no dimensions, and depends solely on the mass of the body. Three types: Inertia of Rest, Inertia of Motion, and Inertia of Direction. Understanding inertia is essential to Newton's First Law and accounts for 1–2 NEET questions each cycle.

Quick Revision Cards

Concept → Trap → Example

1) Inertia — Core Definition

Must Know

Inertia is the inherent property of all bodies by which they cannot change their state of rest or uniform motion in a straight line on their own. It depends only on mass.

  • Inertia ∝ mass (more massive = more inertia)
  • No units, no dimensions — it is a property, not a quantity
  • Two bodies of equal mass have equal inertia regardless of velocity
Example (NEET-style)A loaded truck takes much longer to stop or accelerate than a bicycle of equal speed — higher mass means higher inertia.

2) Inertia of Rest

Exam Favourite

Inability of a body to change its state of rest by itself. A body at rest stays at rest unless an external force acts.

  • Passenger thrown backward when bus starts suddenly
  • Coin falls into glass when card is struck away quickly
  • Dust flies off beaten carpet — dust stays, carpet moves
Example (NEET-style)In the coin–glass experiment: the card is struck so fast that only the card moves (due to external force from finger). The coin cannot start moving on its own (inertia of rest) and falls into the glass under gravity.

3) Inertia of Motion

Common Trap

Inability of a body to change its state of uniform motion by itself. A body in motion continues moving unless acted upon.

  • Passenger thrown forward when bus stops suddenly
  • Athlete runs before a long jump to add velocity
  • Person jumping from moving bus continues forward and runs
Example (NEET-style)When a moving bus brakes suddenly, the lower body stops with the bus, but the upper body continues forward due to inertia of motion — resulting in a forward jerk.

4) Inertia of Direction

Less Common but Asked

Inability of a body to change its direction of motion by itself. Once in circular motion, the body 'wants' to continue tangentially.

  • Stone tied to string flies off tangentially when string breaks
  • Rotating wheel throws mud tangentially
  • Car passenger thrown outward when car rounds a curve suddenly
Example (NEET-style)When a stone on a string is whirled and the string snaps, the stone flies off tangent to the circle — not outward radially. This is inertia of direction in action.

Curriculum Bridging — US vs NEET

Key differences between CBSE/NEET and US AP Physics syllabus for this topic.

Three-Type Classification of Inertia

US AP Physics does not categorise inertia into rest, motion, and direction types.

  • NEET expects precise definition and example mapping for each type
  • Terminology like 'inability to change state' is NCERT-specific — learn exact phrasing

Inertia as Non-Quantifiable Property

AP Physics may imply inertia is synonymous with mass, but NEET distinguishes: inertia is the property, mass is its measure.

  • Do not confuse inertia (property) with mass (physical quantity with units)
  • NEET MCQs ask: 'What are the units of inertia?' — answer: none

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
What is the difference between inertia and mass?
Mass is a physical quantity (measured in kg) that quantifies the amount of matter in a body. Inertia is the property by which a body resists change in its state. Mass is the measure of inertia: greater the mass, greater the inertia. But inertia itself has no units or dimensions.
If a body is in motion, does it have more inertia than when at rest?
No. Inertia depends only on mass, not on the state of motion or velocity. A body of 5 kg has the same inertia whether it is stationary or moving at 100 m/s. This is a very common NEET trap — do not confuse momentum (which depends on velocity) with inertia.
Why does a bullet make a clean hole in glass while a ball shatters it?
A bullet moves at very high speed; its contact time with the glass is extremely small. In that small time, motion is transmitted only to the small area of contact (inertia of rest of the larger glass region). A slow-moving ball has a longer contact time, transmitting the force to the entire pane — causing it to shatter. This is a classic inertia of rest application.
How many marks does inertia typically fetch in NEET?
Inertia as a standalone topic typically yields 1 question (4 marks) per NEET paper. However, inertia concepts underpin Newton's First Law questions, collision scenarios, and more — making mastery high-leverage.
