Conservation of Linear Momentum – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

If no external force acts on a system (called isolated) of constant mass, the total momentum of the system remains constant with time. Conservation of linear momentum is equivalent to Newton's third law of motion.

1) The Core Principle

Theory Focus

When the net external force on a system is zero, its total linear momentum remains conserved.

  • Fext = 0
  • ΔP = 0 => Pinitial = Pfinal
Example (NEET-style)A bomb exploding in mid-air distributes its momentum strictly such that the vector sum of all fragment momenta equals the original momentum.

2) Gun Recoil

Applied Concept

The recoil velocity of a gun fired horizontally is derived directly from the conservation of momentum.

  • v_G = -(m_B/m_G) * v_B
  • The minus sign means the gun recoils opposite to the bullet velocity.
Example (NEET-style)If a 5 kg gun fires a 10 g bullet at 500 m/s, the gun recoils at 1 m/s backwards.

Does momentum conserve in inelastic collisions?
Yes, linear momentum is conserved in all collisions, whether elastic or inelastic, as long as external forces are zero.
