Types of Friction – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Friction is classified into static, kinetic (sliding), and rolling types based on the relative motion between surfaces. Static friction is self-adjusting and acts up to a maximum (limiting friction Fl = μs R) before motion starts. Once motion begins, kinetic friction Fk = μk R acts — always less than limiting friction (μk < μs). Rolling friction (F = μr R/r) is the smallest of the three. NEET tests all three types — their formulas, relative magnitudes, and coefficient properties — in almost every paper.

NEET Mastery Strategy

1

Memorise the Magnitude Hierarchy Rolling friction < Kinetic friction < Static (Limiting) friction. In formula terms: F_rolling < Fk = μk R < Fl = μs R. This ranking is directly tested in NEET MCQs asking ‘which is largest/smallest’.

2

Static Friction is Self-Adjusting When a body is at rest, Fs = applied force (not μs R). Only at the point of impending motion does Fs = Fl = μs R. The trap: applying Fl = μs R for a body still at rest gives the wrong friction value. First check if motion would occur.

3

Rolling Friction Formula F_rolling = μr R / r, where r is the radius of the rolling body. μr has the dimension of length (metres). This formula shows rolling friction decreases with larger radius — why large wheels (train wheels) have lower rolling friction than small wheels.

Rapid Revision Cards

Concept → Trap → Example

1) Static and Limiting Friction

Must Memorise

Static friction is self-adjusting: Fs = applied force, as long as applied force ≤ μs R. Limiting friction Fl = μs R is the maximum before motion starts. Once motion starts, friction drops to kinetic value Fk = μk R.

  • Fs ≤ μs R (static friction is never more than limiting)
  • Fl = μs R (limiting = maximum static friction)
  • μs = Fl / R (definition of static friction coefficient)
  • Dimension of μs: [M⁰L⁰T⁰] (dimensionless)
  • Static friction does NOT depend on area of contact
Example (NEET-style)Block of mass 5 kg on surface with μs = 0.4 (g = 10 m/s²). Fl = 0.4 × 50 = 20 N. If applied force P = 15 N, the body stays at rest and Fs = 15 N (not 20 N). Only if P > 20 N does motion begin.

2) Kinetic and Rolling Friction

Formula Card

Kinetic friction Fk = μk R acts once motion has started; μk < μs always. Rolling friction F = μr R / r acts during rolling; μr has dimension of length. Overall: F_rolling < Fk < Fl.

  • Fk = μk R; μk < μs (always less than limiting coefficient)
  • Kinetic friction is independent of speed (for normal speeds)
  • Kinetic friction is independent of area of contact
  • Rolling friction: F = μr R / r (inversely proportional to radius r)
  • μr has dimension [L] (unit: metres) — NEET trap
Example (NEET-style)μs = 0.5, μk = 0.4 for a 10 kg block (g = 10 m/s²). Fl = 50 N (force needed to start sliding). Once moving, Fk = 40 N (less force needed to maintain sliding). This is why it is harder to start motion than to maintain it.

Rolling Friction Formula Not in AP Physics

AP Physics does not test the rolling friction formula F = μr R / r or the fact that μr has dimensions of length. NEET Class 11 explicitly includes this and uses it in problems involving wheels, spheres, and cylinders.

  • Memorise: F_rolling = μr R / r with μr in metres.
  • Large-radius wheels have smaller rolling friction — hence train wheels are large.

Self-Adjusting Nature Tested Differently

AP Physics states that static friction adjusts up to its maximum. NEET MCQs directly test statements like ‘static friction is a self-adjusting force’ or ask for the friction force when a body is at rest under a force less than limiting friction.

  • When body is at rest with applied force P < Fl: answer is Fs = P.
  • Applying Fl = μs R to a static body under P < Fl is the #1 error in NEET Friction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Why is kinetic friction less than static friction?
When a body is at rest, surface irregularities have time to interlock deeply and molecular bonds form at more contact points. When motion starts, irregularities of one surface have little time to get locked into those of the other, so the resistive force is lower. This is quantified as μk < μs.
Is rolling friction truly zero for a rigid body on a rigid surface?
In the ideal rigid-body limit, a rolling contact point has zero velocity relative to the surface, so no sliding occurs and rolling friction would be zero. In practice, both the body and surface deform slightly at the contact zone, creating energy losses that manifest as rolling friction. This is why tyres heated up: real rolling friction is non-zero.
What is the dimension of the coefficient of rolling friction μr?
μr has the dimension of length [L] and is measured in metres. This is because the rolling friction formula is F = μr R / r: for F and R to have the same dimensions, μr must carry the dimension of r (length). This is a direct NEET MCQ trap because students assume all friction coefficients are dimensionless.
Can static friction exceed limiting friction?
No. Limiting friction is the maximum value of static friction. If the applied force exceeds Fl = μs R, the body begins to move and friction drops to kinetic value Fk. Static friction can be anywhere from zero up to Fl, never above it.
