Velocity at Bottom of Rough Wedge вАУ Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

A body starts from rest at the top of a rough inclined wedge of height h and slides to the bottom. Using the work-energy theorem: the gain in kinetic energy equals the loss in potential energy minus the energy lost to friction (FL, where F = friction force and L = length of incline). The result is: ¬љmv¬≤ = mgh вИТ FL, giving v = вИЪ[2(mgh вИТ FL)/m] = вИЪ[2gh вИТ 2FL/m]. This is the energy method for finding speed at the bottom of any rough incline.
