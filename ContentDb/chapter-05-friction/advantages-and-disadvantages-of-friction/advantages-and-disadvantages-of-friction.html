Advantages and Disadvantages of Friction – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Friction is aptly called a ‘necessary evil’: it is indispensable for walking, braking, writing, holding objects, and belt-driven machinery, yet it simultaneously causes energy dissipation as heat, wear and tear of moving parts, and reduced machine efficiency. Methods to reduce friction include lubrication, polishing, streamlining, and ball bearings. NEET tests this topic through scenario-based MCQs and statement-classification questions identifying advantages vs disadvantages.

1) Advantages of Friction

Direct NEET List

Five key advantages: (1) Walking possible due to friction between feet and ground. (2) Bodies stick together (holding objects, tyres grip road). (3) Brakes work by friction. (4) Writing is impossible without friction. (5) Belt-driven machine part-to-part motion transfer via friction.

  • Walking: foot pushes ground backward; friction pushes foot forward
  • Sticking: nails, screws, adhesives rely on friction
  • Brakes: brake pad pressing rotor creates kinetic friction to decelerate
  • Writing: chalk/pen moves by friction against board/paper
  • Belt drives: pulley-belt contact transmits mechanical power via friction
Example (NEET-style)A person slips on ice (very low μs) because there is insufficient friction for the foot-push mechanism. On rough ground (μs ≈ 0.7), the same walking effort produces safe forward motion.

2) Disadvantages and Reduction Methods

Must Memorise

Three disadvantages: energy loss as heat (reduced efficiency), wear and tear (shorter machine lifetime), heat production damages parts. Reduction methods: polishing, lubrication, streamlining, ball bearings (converts sliding to rolling), material selection.

  • Energy loss: friction force × sliding distance = heat generated (irreversible)
  • Wear: abrasive friction gradually removes material — engine parts need replacement
  • Heat damage: brakes overheat, piston rings wear out
  • Ball bearings: sliding friction (μk R) replaced by rolling friction (μr R/r); reduces friction by ~100×
  • Lubrication: oil layer separates surfaces, reducing direct contact
Example (NEET-style)Car engine oil reduces friction between piston and cylinder wall from μk ≈ 0.3 (dry) to near 0.005 (lubricated), cutting friction force by ~60× and preventing both wear and overheating.

US Curriculum Gaps

How the AP Physics syllabus differs from NEET on advantages and disadvantages of friction.

Not a Standalone Topic in AP Physics

AP Physics does not test advantages and disadvantages of friction as a separate category. NEET Class 11 includes this explicitly from NCERT Chapter 5. Students from the US curriculum need to deliberately memorise NCERT’s lists.

  • Write out all 5 advantages and 3 disadvantages from memory at least once before the exam.
  • NEET MCQ will often include a plausible distractor not in NCERT’s list.

Ball Bearings Mechanism

NEET tests the mechanism of ball bearings: they convert sliding friction into rolling friction, thereby reducing the resistive force. AP Physics may mention bearings in passing but does not test the reduction principle.

  • Sliding friction Fk = μk R ≈ 0.3R; rolling friction ≈ μr R/r ≈ 0.01R for typical bearings.
  • Ball bearings reduce friction by about 100× compared to unlubricated sliding contact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Why is friction called a necessary evil?
Friction is necessary because without it, walking, writing, braking, and virtually all mechanical transmission would be impossible. Yet it is also an evil because it wastes energy, causes heating, and wears out machine parts. Both faces are real and unavoidable in practical systems.
How does lubrication reduce friction?
A lubricant (oil, grease) forms a thin fluid layer between the two solid surfaces, preventing direct surface-to-surface contact. The solid-solid friction force is replaced by internal viscous friction within the fluid, which is orders of magnitude smaller. This also removes the electrostatic bonding component of dry friction.
If friction is harmful, why not eliminate it completely?
Complete elimination of friction is impossible (rolling friction is always non-zero in practice) and undesirable. Without friction, vehicles cannot brake, people cannot walk, and tools cannot grip. Engineering practice minimises harmful friction (in engines) while preserving useful friction (in brakes, tyres, conveyor belts).
What does streamlining a body do to friction?
Streamlining reduces air resistance (fluid friction / drag) by giving the body a shape that allows smooth laminar airflow rather than turbulent separation. This is why aeroplanes, cars, and fish have tapered, elongated shapes. It does not affect solid surface friction directly.
