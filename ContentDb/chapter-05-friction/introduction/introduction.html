Introduction – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Friction is the resistive contact force that opposes relative motion or the tendency of motion between two surfaces. At the atomic level, it arises from electromagnetic bonding between surface molecules and from mechanical interlocking of surface irregularities. Every friction problem in NEET ultimately reduces to identifying the correct friction type, writing the correct expression (F ≤ μs R or Fk = μk R), and using Newton’s laws. The conceptual foundation laid in this topic underpins every calculation in the chapter.

NEET Mastery Strategy

1

Lock In the Definition Friction is the force that opposes relative motion or tendency of motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surface and opposite to intended motion. Both parts of this definition appear in NEET MCQs.

2

Understand the Molecular Origin Friction arises from electromagnetic bonding between surface molecules and interlocking of surface irregularities. Even polished surfaces have microscopic asperities. This is why friction is independent of apparent contact area (asperity contact area remains roughly constant).

3

Distinguish from Normal Force The normal reaction R is perpendicular to the surface; friction is parallel. NEET frequently presents free-body diagrams where students must correctly place both vectors. Confusing their directions is the #1 error in friction problems.

1) Friction: Core Definition

Must Memorise

Friction is the resistive force that opposes relative motion or tendency of motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surface, opposite to the direction of intended motion.

  • Acts parallel to the surface (never perpendicular)
  • Opposes intended or actual relative motion
  • Arises from molecular bonding + surface irregularity interlocking
  • Independent of apparent contact area (key NEET trap)
  • Depends on nature of surfaces and normal reaction
Example (NEET-style)A book at rest on a table, with no applied force, has zero friction. Apply a horizontal force P = 5 N; static friction = 5 N (opposing P). Friction ≠ normal force — it is parallel to the surface.

2) Why Friction is Independent of Area

Concept Trap

Increasing contact area does not increase the real area of asperity contact proportionally — the number of contact points does not multiply. Hence, friction force is independent of apparent (geometric) contact area.

  • Real contact area (asperities) ≪ apparent area
  • For a given weight, asperity contact remains roughly constant regardless of face orientation
  • This is why a brick on its face vs its edge gives the same limiting friction
  • NEET MCQ trap: changing contact area does NOT change friction coefficient or limiting friction
Example (NEET-style)A 1 kg block has the same limiting friction whether placed on its large face (area = 200 cm²) or small face (area = 50 cm²), since normal reaction and μs are unchanged.

US Curriculum Gaps

How the AP Physics C treatment compares with NEET scope.

Area Independence Emphasis

NEET frequently tests the qualitative statement that friction is independent of area. AP Physics includes this but tends to focus on numerical applications rather than the conceptual trap.

  • Memorise: friction force does NOT change if you orient a block on a different face.
  • NEET MCQ pattern: ‘If the area of contact is doubled, the coefficient of friction is — unchanged.’

Direction of Friction Vector

AP Physics and NEET both require correct direction assignment in FBDs, but NEET MCQs more frequently present scenarios where the direction of intended motion is ambiguous, testing whether students can assign friction correctly.

  • Friction opposes TENDENCY of motion if the object is static.
  • Friction opposes ACTUAL relative motion if the object is moving.

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Is friction always a disadvantage?
No. Friction is a necessary evil: it enables walking, vehicle braking, writing, belt drives, and many other essential functions. Yet it also causes energy loss, wear, and heat generation in machinery. NEET specifically asks about both advantages and disadvantages.
Does the direction of friction always oppose motion?
Friction opposes relative motion or tendency of relative motion. In static cases it opposes the tendency to slide. However, friction can be the cause of forward motion (e.g., for a person walking: the friction on the foot is forward, enabling the person to move). The misconception that friction always opposes motion is specifically addressed in the NEET syllabus.
Why doesn’t friction depend on contact area?
The actual contact area (between asperities) does not scale linearly with apparent area. When you place a block on its larger face, there are more contact points but each carries less load; on the smaller face, fewer points carrying more load. The total resistance remains roughly the same. This is why μs and the coefficient of friction are defined purely in terms of the ratio Fl / R — independent of geometry.
What is the SI unit of friction force?
Newton (N). Friction is a force and shares all properties of forces: it is a vector with direction parallel to the surface, measured in newtons, and has dimensions [MLT⁻²]. The coefficient of friction (μs, μk) is dimensionless (no units).
