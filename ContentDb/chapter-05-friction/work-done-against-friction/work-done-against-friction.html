Work Done Against Friction – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Friction is a dissipative force; work is done against it whenever a body moves on a rough surface. On a horizontal surface: W = μmg × s (work against kinetic friction = Fk × displacement). On a rough inclined surface moving up with distance s: W = mgs(sinθ + μ cosθ). NEET tests these formulas both standalone and as part of energy-dissipation problems. Understanding that work done against friction equals the mechanical energy converted to heat is central to energy chapter linkages.

NEET Mastery Strategy

1

Learn Inclined Formula First For uniform motion up rough incline: applied force = mg(sinθ + μ cosθ). Work done = force × distance = mgs(sinθ + μ cosθ). Setting θ = 0 gives the horizontal case W = μmgs. Derive horizontally as a special case to avoid memorising two separate formulas.

2

Understand the Energy Split W_total = W_against_gravity + W_against_friction = mgs sinθ + μmgs cosθ. Gravity component stores as PE; friction component converts to heat. NEET energy questions test this split explicitly.

3

Know the Three Dependences Work done depends on: (i) weight of body (mg), (ii) nature and material of surfaces (μ), (iii) distance moved (s). It does NOT depend on velocity or time. This is a direct NEET conceptual MCQ.

1) Work Done on Rough Inclined Plane

Must Memorise

For uniform motion up a rough inclined plane of angle θ through distance s: Applied force F_app = mg(sinθ + μ cosθ). Work done by this force = F_app × s = mgs(sinθ + μ cosθ). Of this: mgs sinθ goes against gravity (stored as PE), μmgs cosθ goes against friction (converted to heat).

  • W_total = mgs(sinθ + μ cosθ) for uniform motion up rough incline
  • W_gravity = mgs sinθ (stored as gravitational PE)
  • W_friction = μmgs cosθ (dissipated as heat)
  • Normal reaction R = mg cosθ; kinetic friction Fk = μmg cosθ
  • Work done against friction = Fk × s = μmg cosθ × s
Example (NEET-style)m = 4 kg, θ = 30°, μ = 0.25, s = 5 m, g = 10 m/s². W = 4×10×5×(sin30° + 0.25 cos30°) = 200×(0.5+0.25×0.866) = 200×(0.5+0.217) = 200×0.717 = 143.4 J.

2) Work Done on Horizontal Rough Surface

Formula Card

Setting θ = 0 in the inclined formula: W = μmgs. This equals kinetic friction force (Fk = μmg) × distance s. Work depends on: (i) weight mg, (ii) coefficient μ (material), (iii) distance s. It does NOT depend on velocity, time, or area of contact. This is a frequent NEET conceptual MCQ.

  • W = μmgs on horizontal surface (all work goes to heat)
  • Power dissipated by friction = Fk × v = μmgv
  • Heat generated = work done against friction = μmgs (always positive)
  • W is proportional to s (linear, not s²)
  • W does NOT depend on the velocity at which the block moves
Example (NEET-style)m = 10 kg, μ = 0.4, s = 8 m, g = 10 m/s². W = 0.4×10×10×8 = 320 J. All 320 J converts to heat. If the block was moving at 4 m/s, friction power = μmg×v = 40×4 = 160 W.

US Curriculum Gaps

How AP Physics and US curricula compare to NEET scope on work done against friction.

Sign Convention: W_by vs. W_against Friction

AP Physics uses W_by_friction = −μmgs (negative, since friction opposes motion). NEET MCQs ask for ‘work done AGAINST friction’ = +μmgs (positive magnitude). Both conventions represent the same energy dissipation but answer choices differ. Read the question carefully to identify which convention is expected.

  • W_by_friction = −μmgs (AP convention, in work-energy theorem).
  • W_against_friction = +μmgs (NEET convention, energy dissipated).

Three Dependences of W Tested in NEET

NEET explicitly tests that work done against friction depends on weight, μ, and distance — and NOT on velocity or cross-sectional area. AP Physics covers this indirectly; NEET makes it a direct assertion MCQ. 'If the block is moved at twice the velocity, work done against friction is —' must be answered: unchanged per unit distance (but power doubles).

  • W against friction = μmgs: independent of velocity.
  • Power against friction = μmgv: proportional to velocity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Is work done against friction the same as heat generated?
Yes. By the work-energy theorem, when a block moves at constant velocity on a rough surface, all the work done by the applied force goes against friction and is converted to heat (and sound). Numerically: heat generated = work done against friction = Fk × displacement = μmgs (horizontal). This equivalence is the basis of many NEET energy-friction numerical problems.
Why is work done against friction path-dependent but not velocity-dependent?
Friction force magnitude = μmg (horizontal) is constant, so W = μmg × path length. Longer path = more work against friction. Velocity does not appear in the work formula (W = F × s, not F × v). However, POWER against friction = F × v = μmg × v is velocity-dependent.
What is the difference between work done by friction and work done against friction?
Work done BY friction on a sliding block is negative: W_by = −μmgs (friction opposes motion, so force and displacement are anti-parallel). Work done AGAINST friction is positive: W_against = +μmgs (it is the work you must do to overcome friction). In energy accounting: W_against = heat generated. NEET questions use 'against friction' to ask for positive energy dissipation.
For a block pushed 5 m forward then pulled 5 m back, is the net work against friction zero?
No. Friction always opposes motion, so work is done against it in both directions. Going forward: W1 = μmg×5. Coming back: W2 = μmg×5. Total = 2μmg×5 = 10μmg J. Net mechanical displacement is zero but total energy dissipated by friction is 10μmg J.
