Sticking of Person With Wall of Rotor – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

A person stands against the inner wall of a cylindrical drum (rotor) rotating about a vertical axis. Centrifugal force (mω²r) presses the person outward against the wall, providing the normal reaction N = mω²r. The resulting friction f = μN = μmω²r acts upward to support the person’s weight. When μmω²r ≥ mg, the floor can be removed and the person remains stuck to the wall. The critical condition gives the minimum angular velocity: ω_min = √(g/μr). This is a standard NEET application combining circular motion, centrifugal force, and friction.

Difficulty⚡
Medium
The conceptual challenge is connecting circular motion (ω²r) to the friction equilibrium. Students strong in circular motion but not in friction applications may forget that the friction must equal mg. Another trap: using linear acceleration a = v²/r instead of ω²r when deriving N. Both give the same N = mv²/r = mω²r, but must be consistent in the final formula.

NEET Mastery Strategy

1

Draw the FBD in the Rotating Frame In the rotor frame (non-inertial): centrifugal force mω²r acts outward (radially outward), pressing person against outer wall. Normal reaction N = mω²r (inward, from wall). Friction f = μN = μmω²r acts upward. Weight mg downward. Equilibrium: μmω²r ≥ mg ⇒ ω ≥ √(g/μr).

2

Derive v_min and ω_min ω_min = √(g/μr). Linear speed: v_min = ω_min × r = r√(g/μr) = √(gr/μ). Knowing both forms is NEET-important since some problems give v and others give ω.

3

Check Dependence on r ω_min = √(g/μr): larger radius ⇒ smaller minimum angular velocity. v_min = √(gr/μ): larger radius ⇒ larger minimum linear speed. This counterintuitive r-dependence is frequently tested as a conceptual NEET question.

1) Rotor Minimum Speed Derivation

Must Memorise

Person of mass m inside rotor of radius r rotating at ω (rad/s). In rotating frame: centrifugal force F_c = mω²r (outward). Normal reaction N = mω²r (by wall, inward). Friction f = μN = μmω²r (upward). Condition: f ≥ mg ⇒ μmω²r ≥ mg ⇒ ω² ≥ g/(μr) ⇒ ω_min = √(g/μr).

  • N = mω²r (centrifugal force provides normal reaction)
  • f = μN = μmω²r (vertical friction)
  • ω_min = √(g/μr)
  • v_min = √(gr/μ)
  • Mass m cancels: ω_min independent of mass
Example (NEET-style)μ = 0.5, r = 2 m, g = 10 m/s². ω_min = √(10/(0.5×2)) = √10 ≈ 3.16 rad/s. v_min = √(10×2/0.5) = √40 ≈ 6.32 m/s.

Rotor Application Not Standard in AP Physics

AP Physics studies circular motion and friction independently but does not combine them in the rotor/death-cylinder context. The formula ω_min = √(g/μr) and the FBD with vertical friction opposing gravity in a rotating system are NEET-specific applications that must be practised explicitly.

  • Centrifugal force in rotating frame provides N; f = μN must support mg.
  • r-dependence: ω_min ∝ 1/√r; v_min ∝ √r. Know both trends.

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Why does the minimum angular velocity not depend on the person’s mass?
The equilibrium condition is μmω²r = mg. Dividing both sides by m gives μω²r = g, so ω_min = √(g/μr). The mass m cancels completely. Both the centrifugal force (pressing the person outward) and the person’s weight are proportional to m, so their ratio is mass-independent.
What is the real-world example of this problem?
The ‘Death Cylinder’ or ‘Rotor Ride’ in amusement parks works on this principle. The drum spins fast enough that friction between the rough drum wall and riders’ clothing/bodies supports them against gravity. Once the ride reaches ω_min, the floor can be lowered and riders remain pinned to the wall.
How is this problem different from the cart-block sticking problem?
In the cart-block problem, the horizontal pressing force is provided by linear acceleration (pseudo-force ma in the cart’s frame). In the rotor problem, the horizontal pressing force is centrifugal force mω²r in the rotating frame. Both problems have the same FBD structure: horizontal normal force → vertical friction → supports vertical weight. The formula analog is a_min = g/μ (cart) vs. ω_min = √(g/μr) (rotor).
