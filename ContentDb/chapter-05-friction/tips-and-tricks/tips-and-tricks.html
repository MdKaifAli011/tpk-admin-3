Tips and Tricks – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Key facts and conceptual highlights for friction as tested in NEET. Core rules: friction is independent of contact area but depends on the nature of surfaces and the normal reaction. The ordering μ_rolling < μ_sliding < μ_static must be memorised. Friction is a non-conservative force (energy dissipated as heat). Static friction is self-adjusting up to its maximum value (μ_s N). Kinetic friction is constant regardless of speed, while rolling friction is smallest. These conceptual rules appear as NEET MCQs frequently.

NEET Mastery Strategy

1

Memorise the μ Ordering μ_rolling < μ_sliding (kinetic) < μ_static. Rolling friction is smallest (ball/wheel rolls on surface). Static friction is the largest category. Among static, kinetic, and rolling: rolling < kinetic < static. Always. This order is directly testable.

2

Area-Independence Rule Friction does NOT depend on the area of contact. F_friction = μN. Period. NEET often presents statements like ‘increasing contact area increases friction’ — this is FALSE. The reason: more area ⇒ less pressure per unit area ⇒ same total N ⇒ same friction.

3

Non-Conservative Nature Friction is non-conservative: path matters; energy is dissipated as heat. Friction can do negative work (when acting opposite to displacement), positive work (e.g., friction on a book in an accelerating car), or even zero work (static friction when surface is not moving). NEET tests all three cases.

1) Friction: 10 NEET Conceptual Rules

Must Memorise

1) Friction is non-conservative (dissipates energy as heat). 2) μ ordering: μ_rolling < μ_sliding < μ_static. 3) Friction is independent of area of contact. 4) Friction depends on the nature of the surfaces and the normal reaction. 5) Static friction is self-adjusting (0 to μ_s N). 6) Kinetic friction is ~constant, independent of speed. 7) f_limiting = μ_s N (maximum static friction). 8) f_kinetic = μ_k N. 9) Angle of friction = angle of repose = arctan(μ). 10) Friction can act in any tangential direction, not just opposing motion (e.g., static friction drives car forward).

  • μ_rolling < μ_k < μ_s (always)
  • Friction force ≠ f(area): depends only on N and surface nature
  • Non-conservative: friction converts KE to heat
  • Static friction is self-adjusting up to μ_s N
  • Kinetic friction is constant (independent of speed in simple model)
Example (NEET-style)NEET MCQ: friction between block and surface is doubled if contact area is doubled. Answer: FALSE. Friction = μN; N is unchanged, area is unchanged in its effect on friction; F_friction unchanged.

US Curriculum Gaps

How AP Physics compares to NEET on friction conceptual rules.

μ Ordering Less Emphasised in AP Physics

AP Physics covers static and kinetic friction but rarely demands memorisation of the ordering μ_rolling < μ_sliding < μ_static as a standalone test item. NEET frequently presents statements like ‘rolling friction is the largest type’ (FALSE) or ‘static friction is always greater than kinetic friction’ (generally TRUE). NRI students should commit this ordering to memory.

  • Rolling < Kinetic < Static: the order of increasing coefficient of friction.
  • Area-independence: pressing surface harder does NOT increase friction (only increasing N does).

Frequently Asked Questions

Notes · Downloads · Revision · Important Questions
Why is friction independent of area of contact?
Microscopically, friction arises from adhesion at actual contact points (asperities). A larger nominal area does not increase the number of actual contact asperities — the same weight is distributed over a larger area with lower pressure per asperity. The result is the same total friction force. Phenomenologically: F = μN. The area A does not appear, confirming independence.
Can friction do positive work?
Yes. Friction can do positive work when it acts in the direction of displacement. Example: a book resting on an accelerating truck. Static friction from the truck floor pulls the book forward, doing positive work on the book (increasing its KE). Friction is non-conservative but not always ‘negative’ in terms of work done.
Why is μ_rolling < μ_sliding?
Rolling contact involves microscopic deformation and recovery cycles rather than sustained sliding. The energy lost per contact area in rolling is much less than in sliding (where all asperities are continuously sheared). Hence the effective friction coefficient for rolling is much smaller. Ball bearings and tyres exploit this: rolling reduces friction compared to a sliding block.
