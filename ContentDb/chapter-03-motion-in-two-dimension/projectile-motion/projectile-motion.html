Projectile Motion – Complete Notes, Revision, Important Questions & Downloads

Motion of a body which is in flight through the atmosphere under the effect of gravity alone and is not being propelled by any fuel. This topic covers Introduction of Projectile Motion, Assumptions of Projectile Motion, Principle of Physical Independence of Motions and Types of Projectile Motion. Key principles include: If force acting on a particle is oblique with initial velocity, the motion of particle is called projectile motion; The trajectory of a projectile moving under constant acceleration is a parabola. In NEET Physics, Projectile Motion is tested directly and as part of multi-concept problems. Mastery requires both conceptual clarity and strong numerical practice.

1) Core Concept: Projectile Motion

Must Know

Projectile Motion is a key pillar of Motion In Two Dimension in NEET Physics. Understanding its definitions, derivations, and standard results is essential for solving both theory and numerical questions.

  • If force acting on a particle is oblique with initial velocity, the motion of particle is called projectile motion
  • The trajectory of a projectile moving under constant acceleration is a parabola
  • No resistance due to air

2) NEET Traps to Avoid

Exam Alert

NEET setters target conceptual gaps in Projectile Motion. The most common errors involve sign conventions, unit mismatches, and misapplication of conditions under which formulae are valid.

  • If force acting on a particle is oblique with initial velocity, the motion of particle is called projectile motion
  • Horizontal motion is uniform motion
  • Vertical motion is uniformly accelerated or retarded motion
